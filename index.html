The Problem

AI Agents Are Spending
Real Money. With Zero Control.

Enterprises are deploying thousands of autonomous AI agents that browse the web, call APIs, and execute transactions: with no governance layer between them and the company's finances.

🔍
No Visibility
VP Engineering can't see what their agent fleet is spending, when, or why: no dashboard, no logs, no alerts.
⚠️
No Policy Enforcement
Any agent can call any API, pay any destination, at any amount: no spend limits, no allowlists, no approval workflows.
🎯
Prompt Injection Risk
A malicious payload in web content can redirect a research agent to drain wallets or pay attacker-controlled destinations.
📋
No Audit Trail
Finance and Compliance have no cryptographic record of agent transactions: making SOC 2, EU AI Act compliance impossible.
The Solution

The Financial Control Layer
for AI Agents

Tutelum sits between your AI agents and every payment they make: enforcing policy, executing transactions, and creating an immutable record of everything that happens.

One pay() interface: x402, crypto, card, ACH handled automatically
Policy engine runs server-side on every transaction: cannot be bypassed
Cryptographically signed audit log: SOC 2 & EU AI Act ready
Hardware-enforced spend limits via Turnkey TEE: survives a compromised server

Security Architecture

Governance That Cannot Be Bypassed

Dual-layer enforcement: the application layer handles business rules; Turnkey's hardware TEE enforces hard limits even if Tutelum's servers are compromised.

Application Layer
✓Complex business rules & cross-agent budgets
✓Behavioral anomaly detection (async, zero latency)
✓Human approval workflows via Slack / email / webhook
✓Agent identity & risk scoring at scale
✓Payment method routing (x402, USDC, card, ACH)
Turnkey TEE Hardware Layer
🔒Hard spend limits enforced in hardware silicon
🔒Destination allowlists at the key-signing level
🔒Multi-party approval quorums for large transfers
🔒Cryptographic proof of every signing event
🔒Survives a fully compromised application server
CISO Answer: Can an agent spend outside policy if Tutelum's servers are breached? No. The hard spend floor is enforced in hardware by Turnkey's secure enclave: cryptographically verifiable.
